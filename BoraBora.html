Bora-Bora

Bora Bora, the "Pearl of the Pacific," is renowned for its turquoise lagoon, overwater bungalows, and volcanic peaks. To help you prepare, here are some tips on budgeting, packing, safety, and local customs:

Budgeting

  • High season (May–October) is pricier; consider shoulder seasons (April, November) for better deals.
  • Airfare to Papeete and inter-island flights with Air Tahiti should be booked 2–3 months in advance.
  • Expect transfers, activities, and dining at resorts to cost 50–100% more than mainland prices.
  • Use local markets for snacks and souvenirs to save—boutiques at resorts charge premium prices.

Packing Essentials

  • Lightweight, breathable clothing; rash guard or UV-protective swimwear.
  • Reef-safe sunscreen and reef-friendly insect repellent.
  • Reusable water bottle and dry bag for water activities.
  • Power adapter (French outlets, Type E socket).
  • Light rain jacket—brief showers can occur outside dry season.

Safety Tips

  • Stay hydrated and protect against heat; midday sun can be intense.
  • Follow snorkeling guidelines; avoid touching or standing on coral.
  • Use official boat transfers; avoid unlicensed water taxis.
  • Secure valuables in hotel safe; petty theft is rare but possible.

Local Customs

  • Greeting: A light kiss on both cheeks (‘fa’a’ita’) is common; address with Monsieur/Madame.
  • Respect sacred sites: Remove hats and shoes when visiting marae (ceremonial temples).
  • Tipping is not required; round up fares or leave small gratuities (5–10%) for excellent service.
  • Fa’a Samoa: Emphasis on communal harmony—avoid loud disputes in public.

Recommended Activities

  • Snorkeling & Diving: Explore Coral Garden and Lagoonarium.
  • Lagoon Tour: Book half-day tours including shark & ray feeding.
  • Matira Beach: Public beach with stunning sunset views.
  • Mount Otemanu 4×4: Guided off-road adventures for panoramic vistas.

Japan

Japan is renowned for its four distinct seasons, rich traditional culture, cutting‑edge technology, and impeccable hospitality. From neon cityscapes in Tokyo to historic temples in Kyoto, every visit reveals a new side of this endlessly fascinating country.

Transport & Travel Tips

Take advantage of the Japan Rail Pass for unlimited Shinkansen and JR train travel. In cities, use IC cards (Suica, ICOCA) on subways, buses, and convenience stores for seamless transit.

  • Book any long‑distance train tickets and popular attractions well in advance, especially during cherry blossom and autumn foliage seasons.
  • Carry a portable charger and a prepaid IC card to stay connected and tap through transit gates.
  • Download offline maps and translation apps — Wi‑Fi can be spotty in rural areas.
  • Experience a traditional onsen (hot spring). Remember to wash thoroughly before entering the communal bath.
  • Speak softly in public and remove your shoes when entering homes, ryokan, and some temples.
  • Learn basic greetings like “こんにちは” (Konnichiwa) and “ありがとうございます” (Arigatou gozaimasu) — locals appreciate the effort.

Famous Attractions

Tokyo

A high‑tech metropolis with ancient pockets — visit Meiji Shrine, explore Akihabara’s anime shops, and enjoy panoramic views from Tokyo Skytree.

Kyoto

Japan’s historic heart: wander golden Kinkaku‑ji, walk the torii gates at Fushimi Inari, and explore Gion’s geisha district.

Osaka

Known as “Japan’s kitchen,” Osaka delights with street food in Dotonbori, the majestic castle, and a friendly local vibe.

Hokkaido

Japan’s northern wilderness: lavender fields, world‑class ski resorts, and the Sapporo Snow Festival.

Must‑Try Foods

Sushi

Fresh, artfully prepared fish and rice — a quintessential Japanese delicacy.

Ramen

Regional broths from miso in Hokkaido to tonkotsu in Kyushu — slurp away!

China

China is a nation with over 5,000 years of history, blending ancient traditions and cutting‑edge modern culture. From majestic mountains to vibrant megacities, every corner offers something new.

Budgeting

  • Peak season (May–October) costs more; consider April or November for better deals.
  • Book high‑speed train tickets and domestic flights 1–2 months in advance.
  • Street food and local eateries are very affordable compared to tourist restaurants.
  • Public transit (subway/metro) fares range from ¥2–6 per ride—high value for money.

Packing Essentials

  • Layered clothing: northern mornings/evenings can be chilly; southern regions stay humid.
  • Compact umbrella or rain jacket—rainstorms occur outside the dry season.
  • Power bank & universal adapter—China uses Type I/E/F sockets.
  • Basic medications: stomach relief, cold remedies, insect repellent.
  • Face masks & hand sanitizer—for crowded trains or markets.

Safety Tips

  • Keep passport, ID, and valuables locked in hotel safes.
  • Use official taxis or ride‑hail apps; avoid unlicensed cabs.
  • Drink bottled water; avoid tap water unless boiled.
  • Check air quality and weather forecasts—some regions experience haze.

Local Customs

  • Politeness matters: say “Ni hao” (hello), “Xie xie” (thank you), and nod often.
  • At meals: dishes are shared; avoid sticking chopsticks vertically in rice.
  • Gift‑giving: avoid clocks or umbrellas as they symbolize “farewell.”
  • In ethnic minority areas: respect local rituals, dress modestly at temples.

Top Attractions

Beijing

Walk the Great Wall, explore the Forbidden City, and wander Beijing’s historic hutongs.

Tibet (Xizang)

Visit the Potala Palace, Jokhang Temple, and serene Namtso Lake on the Tibetan Plateau.

Must‑Try Foods

Peking Duck

Crispy skin and tender meat served with pancakes, scallions, and sweet bean sauce.

Lanzhou Beef Noodles

Hand‑pulled noodles in a clear, spiced broth topped with tender beef and fresh herbs.

Thailand

Thailand, known as the “Land of Smiles,” blends ancient traditions with modern attractions. From ornate temples and floating markets to neon-lit Bangkok streets and island beaches, it offers endless variety. Thai cuisine—spicy curries, fresh seafood, and sweet desserts—adds flavor to every journey.

Travel Tips for Thailand

  • Visa: Many nationalities enjoy 30-day visa-free entry. Verify rules before traveling.
  • Currency: Thai Baht (THB). ATMs are widespread; small vendors may accept only cash.
  • Dress Code: Cover shoulders and knees when visiting temples; remove shoes before entering.
  • Weather: Tropical climate—hot and humid with occasional rain. Pack light layers and a rain jacket.
  • Etiquette: Wai (palms together) is a respectful greeting. Always remove shoes for indoor and temple visits.
  • Safety: Tap water is not potable—stick to bottled water. Beware of scam tuk-tuks; agree on price first.

Famous Attractions

Bangkok

Thailand’s bustling capital, home to the Grand Palace, Wat Arun, floating markets, and vibrant street food scenes.

Chiang Rai

Northern city famed for its White Temple (Wat Rong Khun), the Blue Temple (Wat Rong Suea Ten), and serene landscapes.

Authentic Food Recommendations

Pad Kra Pao

Stir-fried holy basil with minced pork or chicken, served over rice and topped with a fried egg.

Tom Yum Goong

Spicy‑sour shrimp soup flavored with lemongrass, kaffir lime, galangal, and fresh chilies.

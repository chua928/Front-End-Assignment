Malaysia

Malaysia is a vibrant, diverse destination in Southeast Asia, with modern cities, lush rainforests, stunning beaches, and rich cultural heritage. From Kuala Lumpur’s skyscrapers to Langkawi’s islands and the Cameron Highlands’ tea estates, every traveler finds something to love.

Travel Tips for Malaysia

  • Visa & Entry: Many nationalities get 30‑day visa‑free entry. Check your country’s requirements before booking.
  • Currency: Malaysian Ringgit (MYR). Credit cards are widely accepted, but carry cash for street food and rural areas.
  • Language: Bahasa Malaysia is official, but English is widely spoken, especially in urban centers.
  • Weather: Tropical climate year-round—expect heat and occasional rain. Pack light, breathable clothing and a small umbrella.
  • Etiquette: Dress modestly at religious sites, remove shoes in mosques, and always be polite—“Terima kasih” means “thank you.”
  • Health & Safety: Tap water is generally safe in cities; bottled water is common elsewhere. Mosquito repellent is recommended in jungle and coastal areas.

Famous Attractions

Kuala Lumpur

Home to the iconic Petronas Twin Towers, bustling markets, and a blend of Malay, Chinese, and Indian cultures.

Melaka

A UNESCO heritage city with colonial architecture, Jonker Street night market, and rich Peranakan culture.

Authentic Food Recommendations

Nasi Lemak

Coconut rice with sambal, fried anchovies, peanuts, egg, and cucumber—a national favorite.

Satay

Grilled skewers of marinated meat, served with peanut sauce, cucumber, and ketupat.
